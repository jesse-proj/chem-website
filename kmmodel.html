Kinetic Molecular Model

The Seven Layer Density Column Experiment is an experiment in chemistry. It is an excellent example of a demonstration of how liquids could sit on top of each other. Certain liquids are denser because they contain more material. Other liquids having much lower density occupy lesser volumes as compared to the same volume of them. Due to the molecules in them being closer packed together, liquids with consuming intermolecular interactions have a higher density. Because of their different intermolecular interactions, liquids with different densities in the experiment sink into different layers and are unable to mix completely. This is an excellent demonstration of how the liquid density is understood as a relationship between the mass and the volume.

The liquid state has molecules that are close together, but not locked in place as in a solid. The molecules in a liquid are attracted to each other and they slide past each other. The temperature of the liquid determines how fast the molecules move.

In conclusion, the kinetic molecular model is like an outline. It provides insight into the dynamics of particle motion in matter. It explains why liquids can alter their shape without altering their volume. It also contributes to the understanding of why various kinds of things react differently.
