Seven Layer Density Column Experiment

For our experiment, we will be creating a seven layer density column. A density column is made by layering liquids of different densities. If the neighboring layers are insoluble with one another, then the layers will remain separate unless mixed together. In this experiment, we will be creating a density column using honey, corn syrup, dish soap, water, vegerable oil, isopropyl alcohol, and lamp oil.

The science secret here is density. Every liquid has a density number associated with it. Due to their varying densities, the liquid substances separate from one another—or refuse to mix.

  • Dish Soap
  • Water
